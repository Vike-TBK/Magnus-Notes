Statement of [[Lesere Saraki]], regarding a nightshift at St. Thomas Hospital, London.

**Date of Statement:** [[February 11, 2012]].

**Points of Interest:**
[[Kayleigh Grice]], a doctor of the hospital.
Bodies are evenly burned. Clothing was unharmed.
[[Gerard Keay]] and an unknown man were the burn victims. ([[Strange Old Man (First Aid)]])
[[Gerard Keay]] has eye tattoos on every joint of his body, he also has one over his heart.
These people came from [[St. Mary's Churchyard]]. The place around them shown signs of heat.
[[Strange Old Man (First Aid)]] had now ID.
[[Gerard Keay]] had a zippo lighter with an eye design on it, and an old passport with his ID on it. [[Gerard Keay]] seemed to have traveled a lot.
The [[Strange Old Man (First Aid)]] started whispering words: “Asak” or “Asag”, then “Veepalach” and finally in English “The lightless flame”. ([[Strange Chants]])
The reception was completely empty, every patient was asleep, and every staff member was gone.
A growling/rumbling sounds started happening and the ground started to shake slightly.
The drinks of a vending machine started violently boiling.
The voice of the [[Strange Old Man (First Aid)]] grew louder. His eyes were open and stared at the ceiling.
[[Gerard Keay]] stopped [[Lesere Saraki]] from touching the [[Strange Old Man (First Aid)]], telling her it would have been a bad idea.
[[Gerard Keay]] wanted a small book bound in red leather and a brass pendant he had been wearing. They hadn't brought those in. ([[Magic Books]]) ([[Eye Pendant]])
[[Gerard Keay]] got a scalpel from the storage room, said “Yes. For you, better beholding than the lightless flame”, walked up to the [[Strange Old Man (First Aid)]], muttered something, and stabbed the man in the throat.
The [[Strange Old Man (First Aid)]] then began to "Cremate into ash", even the scalpel disintegrated. [[Gerard Keay]] collected the ashes and gave it to [[Lesere Saraki]] and ask for it to be disposed.
The people in the reception were back.
[[Gerard Keay]] treated for four days before being discharged into the care of his mother.
[[Lesere Saraki]] now gets the feeling of being watched. Not threatened or judged. Just watched. [[Lesere Saraki]] avoids the storage room now.
